The problem

Calls came in across sales, warranty, billing, suppliers, and people asking for the owner by name, while the team was on job sites and couldn't always pick up. The most expensive misses were the leads that called after hours and never called back.

What we built

A receptionist on the main line that answers every call, around the clock, and routes it correctly: real buyers ring the sales reps together so the first available one picks up; warranty and billing go to the office; suppliers are screened; and anyone asking for the owner by name gets one polite qualifying question before anything is offered. The problem

Hundreds of calls a month, plus paid-ad leads that went cold because no one called them back fast enough. The sales manager could not personally catch every call or chase every form fill.

What we built

A two-part engine. On the inbound side, a receptionist answers the main line, routes real buyers straight to the sales manager, and screens out vendors and solicitors. On the outbound side, a speed-to-lead agent calls every paid-ad form lead within minutes of submission, confirms the project, qualifies the fit, and books a callback directly onto the sales manager's calendar.

How it performs

In a single month the receptionist handled 636 calls, routed 110 of them to the sales team, and screened roughly 60 vendor and solicitor calls so the team never wasted a minute on them. About one in five calls traced back to paid ads, which is exactly the spend the speed-to-lead agent now protects by calling those leads back in minutes instead of hours.

Ad leads called back in minutes, not hours, and every real call routed to a closer.

The problem

Leads from phone calls were only as good as whoever remembered to type them in. Real jobs needed to land in their roofing CRM assigned to the right person, not sit in a notebook or an inbox, while spam and wrong numbers cluttered everything.
